Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about living at Glenwood

What are the benefits of a land lease community?

In a land lease community, such as Glenwood, residents own their home and pay a monthly fee to lease the land. They pay no property tax (which is high in Madison County). They get a beautiful home for about half the price of an equivalent home in similar residential neighborhoods. All homes are owner occupied to maintain stability.

What does the monthly fee cover?

The monthly fee covers maintenance of roads, common ground, trees and underground utilities. This fee may increase periodically to cover cost of living increases and other expenses such as maintenance.

How can I get into Glenwood?

Glenwood is an age 55 and above community. Prospective residents will be interviewed by the manager and must pass a credit check. They must sign a lease but can get out of it when they sell their house (no need to pay for the full year as in an apartment lease). Glenwood rules are carefully enforced to maintain the high standards of our community. When an empty lot becomes available, we bring in a new home appropriate for that particular lot and set it up.

How can I buy a home in Glenwood?

It is simpler than buying a residential home.  Legally it is similar to buying a car in that a title is exchanged.  A sales contract is signed, where the price, closing date and other conditions are agreed to.  The seller arranges for an Occupancy Inspection from the city of Alton, where the city checks for working CO detectors in gas homes, smoke detectors in each bedroom and GFCIs.  The seller provides proof of payment of the Madison County Privilege Tax.  This tax is typically about $100/year.  On closing, the buyer receives a mobile home title from the seller or later from the seller‚Äôs lender.   The buyer then takes this to an Illinois Drivers License Bureau (e.g. 20 Terminal Dr, Bethalto, IL, 258-0600) and files it in his name. 

How would I sell my home in Glenwood?

Selling a home is as simple as putting a sign in your yard with flyers. Glenwood has many visitors so it will be seen by many people. You may also list your home on our website for free. Buyers must be approved by Glenwood.

What is the Mobile Home Tax?

Instead of paying real estate taxes, you pay the Madison County Mobile Home Tax.  It is typically about $100/year, depending on the age and size of the home.  If you are 65 or older, you can get a 20% reduction of your bill.  The tax is due July 31 for the current year and tax bills are sent out in June.  You may pay at your bank, by mail or at the Madison County Treasurer‚Äôs Office in Edwardsville.
